01. Abstract

Artificial Intelligence has transitioned from an experimental curiosity to a core creative partner in the music industry. This study examines how these tools democratize creation for bedroom producers while simultaneously challenging traditional concepts of authorship and human "soul."

02. Uses of AI in music

Modern music professionals leverage AI across the entire production cycle to unlock previously unimaginable creative possibilities:

Creation

Generators like Udio and Suno create full tracks with vocals and instruments from text prompts.

Engineering

Tools like Landr and iZotope Ozone provide AI-powered mastering that suggests professional-grade corrections.

Education

Moises allows for "stem separation," isolating vocals or drums from existing tracks for practice and analysis.

03. Societal Impact Matrix

Discovery AI algorithms like "Discover Weekly" allow independent artists to reach global audiences without gatekeepers. Algorithmic bias may force artists to create for "the system" rather than for human expression.

04. Ethical Concerns & Identity

The Deepfake Crisis

The viral track "Heart on My Sleeve" used AI to clone the voices of Drake and The Weeknd, demonstrating how easily a musician's sonic identity can be duplicated without consent.

Creative Sovereignty

Artists like Holly Herndon and Grimes have pioneered using AI as a partner to generate new vocal effects, proving that AI can augment human talent rather than just replace it.
